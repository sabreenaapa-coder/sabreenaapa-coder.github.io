About

Designed with the care, concern and sincerity of a Muslim father.

CastAdhan wasn't built as a product. It was built as a home.

It began with a simple wish: that the adhan would be heard in the house — by the children, by the grandparents, by guests — the way it once marked the rhythm of everyday life. The first units were gifts for family. The software grew the way family software does: one real request at a time. A quieter night-time. A later Asr for a Hanafi aunt. A Suhoor alarm that actually wakes people.

Every unit is prepared and packed by hand in Pembrokeshire, Wales, and dispatched the same day. There is no company behind it in the usual sense — there's a person, and you can reach him.

Will it disappear if I stop?

CastAdhan is built as an open-source project. Even if I stopped working on it tomorrow, the software would remain — for others to improve, maintain and preserve. Your device would keep calling the adhan regardless: it doesn't depend on my servers, or anyone's.
